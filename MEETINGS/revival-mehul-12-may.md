Meeting Introduction and Context: Mehul Mandalia and Muhammad Salman Malik opened the conversation with a brief personal check-in regarding travel and the heat wave in Lahore. The discussion then moved to the upcoming VU conference in June and the requirement to adjust the existing "Fortune Teller" tool for an upcoming satellite event.
Project Objectives for the VU Conference: Mehul Mandalia clarified that the goal is to utilize the "Fortune Teller" tool as an engagement and lead generation mechanism for an informal networking satellite event. The intention is to create a quiz or interaction that helps attendees identify the most relevant people to network with, a process Mehul Mandalia described as "twinning," which allows for pre-matching and pre-qualifying attendees.
System Updates and Rebranding: Sukriti Taneja noted that Shaman had previously addressed bug fixes, and the next technical phase involves hyperlinking website functionalities for new products and performing UI improvements. There is a desire to rename the "Fortune Teller" tool, and if no immediate suggestions are available, Sukriti Taneja agreed to ideate new names and report back.

Privacy and Consent Considerations: Mehul Mandalia clarified that the "twinning" feature should connect attendees with other individuals in their database, rather than with the Moving Walls team, as the team will naturally meet the attendees during the event. Sukriti Taneja noted the necessity of implementing a privacy and consent protocol to inform users that they will be connected with other attendees.
Technical Execution and Data Utilization: Muhammad Salman Malik confirmed that the application can utilize the event's RSVP list, including full names, company information, and roles, to facilitate the networking matching. Muhammad Salman Malik proposed using the existing application framework to support the quiz feature and suggested that by scraping background information and utilizing additional metadata, they can feed the AI more specialized questions and potentially pre-assign meetings based on user input.
Lead Generation Strategy: Mehul Mandalia emphasized that the tool must provide value to the user to avoid appearing as a sales pitch. Users will receive a basic score at the event booth, while the full report will be sent via email to ensure the team can capture lead information in their CRM and track engagement with the report. Muhammad Salman Malik agreed to verify if the system currently supports the automated delivery of these full reports.
Value Generation and User Intel: Muhammad Salman Malik and Mehul Mandalia discussed strategies to provide value to different user segments. For marketers and agencies, they discussed scoring digital ad performance and providing insights on how out-of-home advertising can improve campaign efficiency by 30% to 40%. For media owners, the conversation focused on helping them improve business operations, such as increasing discoverability, managing inventory, and adopting better CRM practices.
Implementation and Prioritization: Muhammad Salman Malik and Mehul Mandalia agreed that the priority is to refine the networking and "twinning" functionality first, followed by the development of the quiz and scoring features. Sukriti Taneja committed to gathering the necessary attendee database and internal data points, such as case studies, to support these efforts, and the team agreed to reconnect at the end of the week to review progress.
Next Steps and Follow-up Meeting: Muhammad Salman Malik will conduct research on the scoring mechanism and requested a meeting with Sukriti Taneja to review the refined plan and address any potential gaps. Sukriti Taneja and Muhammad Salman Malik agreed to coordinate their calendars to schedule this follow-up discussion for the following day.
