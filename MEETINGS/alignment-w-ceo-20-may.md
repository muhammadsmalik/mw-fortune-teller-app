Fortune Teller 2.0 - Strategic Framework and Case Studies: Srikanth Ramachandran proposed a new framework for the "Fortune Teller" tool based on three themes: connected media, incrementality, and relevance encoding. They suggested open-sourcing this knowledge to media owner sales teams so they can use it as a co-pilot to prepare for client meetings by scanning case studies and generating discussion points. Srikanth Ramachandran emphasized that the tool should help users understand business goals and provide solutions based on moving walls science and influence measures.
Fortune Teller 2.0 - Technical Improvements and Networking: Srikanth Ramachandran identified critical issues from the previous version, specifically "hallucinations" where the AI provided inaccurate or repetitive recommendations. To fix this, they proposed pre-processing a database of the 400 to 600 confirmed attendees to accurately identify "twins"—people with similar interests or backgrounds. Muhammad Salman Malik agreed to add a layer of enrichment beyond LinkedIn data, such as shared articles or agency history, to prevent the system from matching everyone to a single high-profile individual.
Fortune Teller 2.0 - User Experience and Self-Service: Srikanth Ramachandran and Mehul Mandalia agreed that the new version must be completely self-service to avoid the heavy "handholding" required during previous events. The interaction should be frictionless, requiring no more than three simple questions, as most data will be pre-populated from LinkedIn. Mehul Mandalia suggested that users scan a code to visit a site directly on their own devices rather than using shared machines.
Fortune Teller 2.0 - Media Owner Scoring Dimensions: Muhammad Salman Malik and Mehul Mandalia brainstormed adding a scoring feature based on five dimensions to evaluate media owners. These include "discoverability" (how easily their media can be found via search or AI), "ease of purchase" (connectivity to demand partners and external marketplaces), and "measurement" (provision of data points). Srikanth Ramachandran noted that these scores should be pre-run and cleaned for known attendees to ensure accuracy.
Fortune Teller 2.0 - Pre-Event Engagement and Scheduling: Hatim Kantawalla and Srikanth Ramachandran discussed using the tool for pre-event outreach. Hatim Kantawalla proposed building a pre-meeting calendar and brief for attendees by matching "twins" before the event starts. Srikanth Ramachandran suggested sending "fortunes" via email to the RSVP list to drive traffic to their booth, where the booth then serves as a physical meeting point for the identified matches to handshake.
Data Preparation and AI Agent Development: Sukriti Taneja committed to finishing a curated list of all attendee names and LinkedIn URLs by the end of the day, as manual matching was required to ensure accuracy that AI could not achieve alone. Muhammad Salman Malik will use this list to create the profiles and will also work on the methodology for the AI agent’s scoring system. Mehul Mandalia requested that Muhammad Salman Malik post samples in the group for feedback to avoid unnecessary calls.
