## What we're building

The existing Fortune Teller app, repurposed as an **insight-led networking + lead-gen tool** for booth attendees. Two mechanics:

1. **Twinning** — pre-match each attendee with another attendee they should meet.
2. **Scoring** — a 5-question self-assessment that produces a 0–100 score, an archetype, and a recommended MW product.

Full personalised report emailed post-event. CRM tracks engagement.

Priority: Twinning first, scoring second.

---

## The Score (keep the math dumb)

- **5 questions per persona**, each answered on a 1–5 scale.
- Score = `sum(answers) × 4` → 0–100. Deterministic, no LLM, no benchmark lookup needed for the number.
- Split into **5 sub-scores** for the email report radar chart.
- At the booth: archetype + headline score only. Email reveals the rest.

**Media Owner dimensions** (1 question each, mapped to MW's 7 products):
1. Discoverability *(→ Studio)*
2. Bookability *(→ Market)*
3. Measurement *(→ Measure)*
4. Yield / Fill *(→ Influence)*
5. Speed of execution *(→ Activate)*

Every weakness in the score = a natural MW product recommendation. No salesy framing needed.

## Question Copy (final, booth-ready)

Designed for fast tap-through on a tablet. Each question has a short stem, a one-line clarifier, and 5 self-locating anchors. Anchor 1 describes a real operating model (not "bad") so respondents answer honestly; anchor 5 describes a state real MW customers already operate in (not aspirational fluff).

### Q1 — Discoverability *(→ Studio)*

**How does a new buyer usually find your inventory?**
*The route from "I've never heard of you" to "show me what you've got."*

1. Word of mouth or cold outreach — they have to know us first
2. We share a PDF rate card or site list when asked
3. Our website has a searchable site finder or map
4. We're listed on one or more programmatic marketplaces
5. Buyers can find, filter, and check live availability across multiple exchanges

### Q2 — Bookability *(→ Market)*

**From "I want to book" to "it's confirmed," how long does it typically take?**
*Measure the elapsed time, not the working time.*

1. Two weeks or more — RFP, negotiation, IO, sign-off
2. About a week — mostly email back-and-forth
3. A few days — templated quotes, faster sign-off
4. Same day — buyers can hold inventory and confirm in hours
5. Minutes — fully self-serve, programmatic guaranteed or PMP

### Q3 — Measurement *(→ Measure)*

**What do you give buyers as proof the campaign ran?**
*Post-campaign reporting — what lands in their inbox.*

1. Proof-of-posting photos and play logs
2. Play logs plus an audience estimate from our own methodology
3. Third-party verified impressions
4. Impressions plus audience composition (demographics, mobility, etc.)
5. Full attribution — impressions, audience, and outcomes tied to buyer KPIs

### Q4 — Yield / Fill *(→ Influence)*

**How is your inventory priced?**
*The honest answer, not the rate card answer.*

1. Fixed rate card — discounts are negotiated case-by-case
2. Rate card with standard discounts by volume or duration
3. Tiered pricing by daypart, season, or location — reviewed quarterly
4. Dynamic pricing on a portion of inventory (e.g. programmatic only)
5. Fully dynamic yield — prices respond to demand automatically across the network

### Q5 — Speed of Execution *(→ Activate)*

**From confirmed booking to creative live on screen — how long?**
*Assume a standard creative, no compliance issues.*

1. Five or more days — manual upload, multiple approvals
2. Two to four days — partial CMS, some manual steps
3. About a day — CMS-driven but human-gated
4. A few hours — automated pipeline with a final review
5. Under an hour — API-driven, no human in the loop

**Copy principles applied:**
- Anchor 1 is never "bad" — it describes a defensible relationship-led model. Keeps respondents honest.
- Jargon (IO, PMP, PG, DSP) appears only in clarifiers, never as the sole signal.
- The clarifier line pre-empts the "well, it depends…" instinct that kills self-assessments.

## Twinning

- Inputs: attendee Name, Designation, Organisation, LinkedIn (from Sukriti's RSVP list).
- **Matching pool = the RSVP list only.** No external attendees, no cross-event matches. If an attendee isn't on the list, they get the score path but no twin.
- Output: **one** high-signal match per attendee, with a 1-line "why you two" generated by LLM from their RSVP profiles + quiz answers.
- All matches are Media Owner ↔ Media Owner. Differentiation comes from geography, format mix (DOOH vs static, transit vs roadside), inventory scale, and product gaps revealed by the quiz — not from persona type.
- Reveal at booth as a tarot-style card flip. Optional, gated behind consent screen.
